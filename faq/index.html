Frequently asked questions.

What the practice does, how engagements work, and where the boundaries sit — particularly around certification, which is the question we are asked most often.

What does The Digital Systems Architect do?

We design the operational systems an organisation runs on — workflow automation, governance dashboards, finance and HR processes — and connect that readiness to market visibility and, where relevant, halal and Muslim-friendly market access. The through-line is that an organisation which runs well but cannot be found, or is visible but not ready to serve, is only half-built.

Do you only work with organisations in Singapore?

No. The practice is based in Singapore and much of the operational systems work is delivered locally, but engagements extend across Japan, the wider region, and organisations entering Muslim consumer markets. Local regulatory context is applied where it genuinely bears on the work rather than assumed by default.

What is a governance dashboard?

An operating view showing status, risk, exceptions, ownership and approval progress across processes — with each figure traceable to a source record, so it can be explained under audit rather than simply asserted. More on governance dashboards.

Do you issue halal certification?

No. Halal certification is always issued by a halal certification body. In Singapore our role is advisory only. In Japan we are able to issue Muslim-friendly certification directly. Muslim-friendly certification and halal certification are not the same thing, and we are careful not to blur them. See halal & Muslim-friendly advisory.

Is the Muslim-Friendly Directory the same as halal certification?

No. Directory participation and self-declaration are not equivalent to halal certification. Each listing states its own status, and diners are asked to review it and make their own enquiries where a requirement is essential to them. See the Muslim-Friendly Directory.

Can you support finance and HR operations?

Yes — billing workflows, reconciliation, management reporting, HR approvals, resource allocation and workload visibility. See finance automation and HR systems.

How do engagements usually start?

With a specific problem rather than a scope document — a process that keeps breaking, a report nobody trusts, a market that cannot be entered yet. The scope follows from what the review finds, which occasionally means concluding that the thing you asked for is not the thing you need.
